CANDLESTICK CHART

Welcome to this page! Learn more about candlestick charts and how to interpret them.

What Is A Candlestick Chart?

A candlestick chart is a type of financial chart used to represent the price movement of securities, derivatives, and currencies. Each candlestick provides key information about market trends for a specific time period, such as a day or hour.

  • **Open Price**: The price at which the asset started trading during the period.
  • **Close Price**: The price at which the asset ended trading during the period.
  • **High Price**: The highest price reached during the period.
  • **Low Price**: The lowest price reached during the period.

How to Read a Candlestick Chart

Candlesticks give a quick visual representation of market trends and patterns:

  • A **green candlestick** (or hollow) indicates a price increase (close > open).
  • A **red candlestick** (or filled) indicates a price decrease (close < open).
  • The **body** of the candlestick represents the difference between the open and close prices.
  • The **wicks (or shadows)** above and below the body show the high and low prices during the period.
  • Look for patterns such as **Doji**, **Hammer**, or **Engulfing**, which can signal market reversals or continuations.
